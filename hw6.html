About

Call of Duty: Black Ops 6 is a 2024 first-person shooter video game co-developed by Treyarch and Raven Software and published by Activision. It is the twenty-first installment of the Call of Duty series and is the seventh main entry in the Black Ops sub-series, following Call of Duty: Black Ops Cold War (2020). Set in 1991, Black Ops 6's single-player story follows rogue operatives Troy Marshall and Frank Woods as they assemble a team of agents to hunt down Pantheon, a paramilitary group that intends to enact a false flag attack on the United States in order to discredit and replace the Deputy Director of the CIA.

Gameplay

Like previous Call of Duty titles, Black Ops 6 is a first-person shooter, featuring a single-player campaign, a multiplayer component, and the cooperative round-based Zombies mode. New gameplay mechanics in Black Ops 6 include omnidirectional movement, which allows players to sprint, dive, and slide in any direction. The game also offers various "Intelligent Movement" settings, which automate certain player actions, including sprinting and mantling.

Plot

In 1991, amidst the start of Operation Desert Storm, CIA operatives Troy Marshall and William "Case" Calderon, along with handler Jane Harrow, are deployed to the Iraq–Kuwait border to extract the Iraqi Minister of Defense Saeed Alawi, but are forced to go off mission when Alawi claims to be targeted by a rogue paramilitary force called "Pantheon".

Zombies

Following the sacrifice of Samantha Maxis to seal the rift between Earth and the "Dark Aether" dimension in 1985, the classified CIA task force Requiem is shut down, with its senior staff—Special Officer Grigori Weaver, Dr. Elizabeth Grey, Major Mackenzie Carver, Dr. Oskar Strauss, and Captain Stoney "Raptor One" Maddox—arrested and imprisoned on Terminus Island, a remote research facility in the Philippine Sea.
